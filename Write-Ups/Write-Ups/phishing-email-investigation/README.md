Investigating a Suspicious Phishing Email
Overview

Phishing is one of the most common attack vectors used by adversaries to gain unauthorized access to corporate environments. These attacks rely heavily on social engineering techniques to manipulate users into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading infected attachments.

This investigation analyzes a suspicious email reported to the security team to determine whether it represented legitimate communication or a phishing attempt. The analysis involved reviewing email header metadata, examining sender information, extracting embedded links, and evaluating potential indicators of compromise.

Multiple phishing indicators were identified during the investigation, including a spoofed sender address impersonating a legitimate organization, the use of urgent language to pressure the recipient, and manipulated links designed to redirect the user to a malicious external destination.

Key Findings

• Spoofed sender address impersonating a legitimate organization
• Social engineering techniques designed to create urgency
• Embedded hyperlinks redirecting to suspicious domains
• Mismatch between sender display name and actual sending domain
• Email classified as Confirmed Phishing Attempt
